# The Twenty-One Precepts of the Living Way

### *Warrior discipline in plain speech — after the spirit of the Dokkōdō*

---

These are **original** precepts for daily practice. They borrow **nothing** verbatim from historical texts; they echo **one** intention: **clarity under pressure**.

1. Do not build your peace on **being right**—build it on **being true**.
2. **Practice** when it is boring; that is when it becomes yours.
3. **Eat** to serve the body, not the anxiety.
4. **Sleep** as surrender, not as escape.
5. Do not **rehearse** arguments in the shower; **speak** or **release**.
6. **Envy** is a map of your hunger—read it; do not **obey** it.
7. **Praise** without flattery; **correct** without cruelty.
8. **Win** without making the loser your enemy forever.
9. **Lose** without making yourself a permanent wound.
10. **Silence** is not always holy; sometimes it is **cowardice**. Know which.
11. **Noise** is not always courage; sometimes it is **panic**. Know which.
12. **Serve** what is larger than your mood.
13. **Cut** one small dishonesty per day—no theater.
14. **Forgive** the past’s ignorance; **do not** rehire it as your advisor.
15. **Train** attention like a blade: **daily**, **lightly**, **seriously**.
16. **Love** without claiming ownership of another’s soul.
17. **Walk** away from games whose prize is **humiliation**.
18. **Stay** where the work is **real**, even if the room is small.
19. **Study** many paths; **pledge** to truth, not to **badges**.
20. When you **do not know**, say so—**that** is strength.
21. **Die** to the story that you must be someone else to be loved—**live** the one life you have.
