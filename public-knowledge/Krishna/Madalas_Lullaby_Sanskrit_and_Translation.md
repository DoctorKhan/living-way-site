# Madālasā Lullaby: Sanskrit Text and Translation

**Source:** *Mārkaṇḍeya Purāṇa*, Madālasopākhyāna, **chapter 25** — *Bālollāpana-gītam* (Ullāpana-gītam). The śloka text below follows the proofread recension at [Sanskrit Documents — Ullāpanagītam](https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_giitaa/ullApanagItam.html) (chapter numbering may appear as 23 in some editions).

The opening **refrain** is widely sung in devotional practice and teaching anthologies; it is not part of ślokas 11–14 in that recension but belongs to the same Madālasā cycle in popular transmission.

---

## The Refrain

**Sanskrit:** शुद्धोऽसि बुद्धोऽसि निरञ्जनोऽसि  
संसारमाया परिवर्जितोऽसि  
संसारस्वप्नं त्यज मोहनिद्रां  
मदालसोल्लापमुवाच पुत्रम्

**Transliteration:** *Śuddhosi buddhosi nirañjanosi* · *Saṃsāramāyā parivarjitosi* · *Saṃsārasvapnaṃ tyaja mohanidrāṃ* · *Madālasollāpamuvāca putram*

**Translation:** You are ever pure, you are awakened, you are spotless. You are free from the māyā of saṃsāra. Abandon the dream of saṃsāra and the sleep of delusion. Thus Madālasā addressed her son.

---

## Śloka 11 — On identity and name

**Sanskrit:** शुद्धोऽसि रे तात न तेऽस्ति नाम  
कृतं हि ते कल्पनयाधुनैव ।  
पञ्चात्मकं देहमिदं तवैत-  
न्नैवास्य त्वं रोदिषि कस्य हेतोः ॥

**Transliteration:** *Śuddhosi re tāta na te ’sti nāma* · *Kṛtaṃ hi te kalpanayādhunaiva* · *Pañcātmakaṃ deham idaṃ tavaitan* · *Naivāsya tvaṃ rodiṣi kasya hetoḥ*

**Translation:** You are pure, my child; you have no name. What is made (as a name) is only a mental construct in this moment. This body of five elements is yours only as appearance; you are not identical with it—why do you weep?

---

## Śloka 12 — On weeping and sense-qualities

**Sanskrit:** न वा भवान्रोदिति वै स्वजन्मा  
शब्दोऽयमासाद्य महीशसूनुम् ।  
विकल्प्यमाना विविधा गुणास्ते  
ऽगुणाश्च भौताः सकलेन्द्रियेषु ॥

**Transliteration:** *Na vā bhavān roditi vai svajanmā* · *Śabdo ’yam āsādya mahīśasūnum* · *Vikalpyamānā vividhā guṇās te* · *’Guṇāś ca bhautāḥ sakalendriyeṣu*

**Translation:** Nor do you (truly) weep, O one whose birth is your own; only this sound reaches you, O son of the lord of the earth. The manifold (qualities) are imagined as yours; the qualities are of the elements, (present) in all the senses.

---

## Śloka 13 — On growth and decay

**Sanskrit:** भूतानि भूतैः परिदुर्बलानि  
वृद्धिं समायान्ति यथेह पुंसः ।  
अन्नाम्बुदानादिभिरेव कस्य  
न तेऽस्ति वृद्धिर्न च तेऽस्ति हानिः ॥

**Transliteration:** *Bhūtāni bhūtaiḥ paridurbalāni* · *Vṛddhiṃ samāyānti yatheha puṃsaḥ* · *Annāmbudānādibhir eva kasya* · *Na te ’sti vṛddhir na ca te ’sti hāniḥ*

**Translation:** Elements are enfeebled by elements; they come to growth as a person’s (body) does here—through food, water, and the like. For whose (sake is that)? For you there is neither increase nor decrease.

---

## Śloka 14 — The body as garment

**Sanskrit:** त्वं कञ्चुके शीर्यमाणे निजेऽस्मिं-  
स्तस्मिंश्च देहे मूढतां मा व्रजेथाः ।  
शुभाशुभैः कर्माभिर्देहमेत-  
नादादिमूढैः कञ्चुकस्तेपि नद्धः ॥

**Transliteration:** *Tvaṃ kañcuke śīryamāṇe nije ’smin* · *Tasmiṃś ca dehe mūḍhatāṃ mā vrajethāḥ* · *Śubhāśubhaiḥ karmābhir deham etan nādādi mūḍhaiḥ kañcukas te ’pi naddhaḥ*

**Translation:** You (dwell) in your own garment as it wears away—in that very body do not fall into delusion. By auspicious and inauspicious actions this body is bound; from the outset the deluded are bound in that garment as well.

---

> **Note:** These ślokas are a concise sample of Queen Madālasā’s non-dual instruction, meant to turn the mind away from identification with body and name. The full *Ullāpana-gītam* continues with further verses in the same chapter.
