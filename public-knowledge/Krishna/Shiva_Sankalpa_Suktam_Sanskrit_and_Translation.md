# Śiva-saṅkalpa Suktam: Vedic Hymn for the Mind

**Source:** These six mantras are the opening of **chapter 34** of the **Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā** (Śukla Yajurveda); they are widely transmitted as the **Śiva-saṅkalpa Upaniṣad**. The text below follows the proofread recension at [Sanskrit Documents — Śivasankalpopaniṣat](https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_upanishhat/shivasankalpopanishad.html). Related or parallel passages also appear in other Vedic rescensions (including Khila and ancillary collections); the title **śiva-saṅkalpa** means an **auspicious resolve**—a prayer that the mind be turned toward good and true intention.

**Refrain (each verse):** *Tan me manaḥ śivasaṅkalpam astu* — “May that mind of mine be of auspicious resolution.”

---

## Key theme

The mind is the instrument through which we experience the world, perform duty (*dharma*), and approach realization. By returning to the refrain, the hymn asks that this faculty itself become *śiva*—auspicious, whole, rightly directed.

## Structure (six movements)

1. **The wanderer** — The mind’s reach in waking and sleep; the “light of lights.”
2. **The performer** — The mind in ritual, work, and ordered action.
3. **The light** — Knowledge, awareness, steadfastness; the inner light without which nothing is done.
4. **The encompassing** — What holds past, present, and future; the sacrifice sustained by seven *hotṛ*s.
5. **The hub** — Where Ṛg, Sāma, and Yajur rest; the woven consciousness of beings.
6. **The charioteer** — The mind guiding persons as a driver guides horses; seated in the heart, swift, unfading.

---

## Mantra 1 — The nature of the mind

**Sanskrit:** यज्जाग्रतो दूरमुदैति दैवं तदु सुप्तस्य तथैवैति ।  
दूरङ्गमं ज्योतिषां ज्योतिरेकं तन्मे मनः शिवसङ्कल्पमस्तु ॥

**Transliteration:** *Yaj jāgrato dūram udaiti daivaṃ tad u suptasya tathaivaiti* · *Dūraṅgamaṃ jyotiṣāṃ jyotir ekaṃ tan me manaḥ śivasaṅkalpam astu*

**Translation:** That which goes far while one is awake, and likewise while one sleeps; that far-going one light of lights—may that mind of mine be of auspicious resolution.

---

## Mantra 2 — The mind in action

**Sanskrit:** येन कर्माण्यपसो मनीषिणो यज्ञे कृण्वन्ति विदथेषु धीराः ।  
यदपूर्वं यक्षमन्तः प्रजानां तन्मे मनः शिवसङ्कल्पमस्तु ॥

**Transliteration:** *Yena karmāṇy apaso manīṣiṇo yajñe kṛṇvanti vidatheṣu dhīrāḥ* · *Yad apūrvaṃ yakṣam antaḥ prajānāṃ tan me manaḥ śivasaṅkalpam astu*

**Translation:** That by which thoughtful workers of rite perform their acts in sacrifice and in sacred assemblies; that which was first, the inner power (*yakṣa*) of creatures—may that mind of mine be of auspicious resolution.

---

## Mantra 3 — The repository of knowledge

**Sanskrit:** यत्प्रज्ञानमुत चेतो धृतिश्च यज्ज्योतिरन्तरमृतं प्रजासु ।  
यस्मान्न ऋते किञ्चन कर्म क्रियते तन्मे मनः शिवसङ्कल्पमस्तु ॥

**Transliteration:** *Yat prajñānam uta ceto dhṛtiś ca yaj jyotir antar amṛtaṃ prajāsu* · *Yasmān na ṛte kiñcana karma kriyate tan me manaḥ śivasaṅkalpam astu*

**Translation:** That which is discernment, mind, and firmness; that immortal inner light among beings; apart from which no action at all is done—may that mind of mine be of auspicious resolution.

---

## Mantra 4 — The controller of time

**Sanskrit:** येनेदं भूतं भुवनं भविष्यत्परिगृहीतममृतेन सर्वम् ।  
येन यज्ञस्तायते सप्तहोता तन्मे मनः शिवसङ्कल्पमस्तु ॥

**Transliteration:** *Yenedaṃ bhūtaṃ bhuvanaṃ bhaviṣyat parigṛhītam amṛtena sarvam* · *Yena yajñas tāyate sapta-hotā tan me manaḥ śivasaṅkalpam astu*

**Translation:** By which this world—what was, what is, and what will be—is all held together in the immortal; by which the sacrifice is upheld with seven *hotṛ*s—may that mind of mine be of auspicious resolution.

---

## Mantra 5 — The foundation of wisdom

**Sanskrit:** यस्मिनृचः साम यजूंषि यस्मिन्प्रतिष्ठिता रथनाभाविवाराः ।  
यस्मिंश्चित्तं सर्वमोतं प्रजानां तन्मे मनः शिवसङ्कल्पमस्तु ॥

**Transliteration:** *Yasmin ṛcaḥ sāma yajūṃṣi yasmin pratiṣṭhitā rathanābhā ivārāḥ* · *Yasmiṃś cittaṃ sarvam otaṃ prajānāṃ tan me manaḥ śivasaṅkalpam astu*

**Translation:** In which Ṛc, Sāman, and Yajus are fixed like spokes about a chariot’s nave; in which the whole consciousness of beings is woven—may that mind of mine be of auspicious resolution.

---

## Mantra 6 — The divine charioteer

**Sanskrit:** सुषारथिरश्वानिव यन्मनुष्यान्नेनीयतेऽभीशुभिर्वाजिन इव ।  
हृत्प्रतिष्ठं यदजिरं जविष्ठं तन्मे मनः शिवसङ्कल्पमस्तु ॥

**Transliteration:** *Suṣārathir aśvān iva yan manuṣyān nenīyate ’bhīśubhir vājina iva* · *Hṛtpratiṣṭhaṃ yad ajiraṃ javiṣṭhaṃ tan me manaḥ śivasaṅkalpam astu*

**Translation:** Like a good charioteer with horses, that by which people are led with reins, as champions (*vājin*) are; that which is established in the heart, unaging, swiftest—may that mind of mine be of auspicious resolution.

---

**Closing (traditional):** *Oṃ śāntiḥ śāntiḥ śāntiḥ*
