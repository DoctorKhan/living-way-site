# The Eternal Way of the Unforced Leader

## (道德經)

### Chapter 1

道可道，非恆道。
Ways that can be spoken are not the eternal way.

名可名，非恆名。
Distinctions we can define are not eternal distinctions.

有名，萬物之母。
With distinction, it spans the world of forms, encompassing all things seen.

無名，萬物之始；
Without distinction, it precedes the world of forms, residing within the eternal unseen.

恆有欲，以觀其徼；
With desire, you observe the boundaries, its limits becoming clear.

故恆無欲，以觀其妙。
Without desire, you observe the inner workings, its nature becoming clear.

異名同謂。
Both of these are different distinctions, yet point to one way.

兩者同出，
For both of these have the same origin.

玄之又玄。
Within the depths of the unseen

眾妙之門。
lies the gateway to all profound mysteries.

### Chapter 2

天下皆知美之為美，
When beauty is distinguished,

斯惡已。
ugliness appears.

皆知善
When goodness is defined,

斯不善矣。
what is not-good is born.

有無之相生也，
Being and non-being create each other.

難易之相成也，
Difficult and easy shape each other.

長短之相刑也，
Long and short measure each other.

高下之相盈也，
High and low balance each other.

音聲之相和也，
Tone and sound weave each other into harmony.

先後之相隨也。
Before and after walk in sequence.

是以聖人居無為之事，
Therefore, the unforced leader moves before distinctions harden into opposites,

行不言之教；
and guides without imposing words.

萬物作焉而不辭，
All things arise, and the leader does not turn away.

生而不有，
They bring forth, but do not possess.

為而不恃，
They act, but do not cling to the act.

功成而弗居。
They accomplish, but do not dwell on accomplishment.

夫唯弗居，是以不去。
Because they do not dwell on it, it does not leave them.

### Chapter 3

不尚賢，使民不爭； 不貴難得之貨，使民不為盜；
When superiority is exalted,
people begin to compete.
When rare things are prized,
people begin to steal.

不見可欲，使民心不亂。
When desire is put on display,
the heart grows restless.

是以聖人之治，虛其心，實其腹，
Therefore, the unforced leader
empties the mind of excess
and fills the belly with what is needed.

弱其志，強其骨。 常使民無知無欲。
They soften striving
and strengthen what endures,
keeping people free
from manufactured knowing
and grasping desire.

使夫智者不敢為也。
Then cleverness does not rush to interfere.

為無為，則無不治。
Practice unforced action,
and all things return to order.

### Chapter 4

道沖而用之或不盈，淵兮似萬物之宗；
The Way is an emptiness that cannot be used up,
a depth that seems the source of all things.

挫其銳，解其紛，和其光，同其塵，湛兮似或存。
It blunts the sharp, unties the tangled,
softens the glare, and settles with the dust.
Deep and still, it seems to remain.

吾不知誰之子，象帝之先。
I do not know whose child it is.
It seems to be there before the first image of the divine.

### Chapter 5

天地不仁，以萬物為芻狗；
Heaven and earth are not sentimental.
They treat all things like straw dogs.

聖人不仁，以百姓為芻狗。
The sages are not sentimental.
They treat the people like straw dogs.

天地之間，其猶橐籥乎？ 虛而不屈，動而愈出。
Is not the space between heaven and earth like a bellows?
Empty, yet never exhausted;
the more it moves, the more it gives.

多言數窮，不如守中。
Too many words run dry.
Better to keep to the center.

### Chapter 6

谷神不死，是謂玄牝。
The valley spirit never dies.
This is called the mysterious feminine.

玄牝之門，是謂天地根。
The gate of the mysterious feminine
is the root of heaven and earth.

緜緜若存，用之不勤。
Unbroken, it seems to remain.
Draw from it,
and it is never exhausted.

### Chapter 7

天長地久。
Heaven endures.
Earth abides.

天地所以能長且久者，以其不自生，
They endure and abide
because they do not live for themselves.

故能長生。 是以聖人後其身而身先；
And so they endure.
Therefore, the unforced leader
places themselves behind,
and comes to the front;

外其身而身存。
lets the self fall away,
and the self is preserved.

非以其無私邪，故能成其私。
Is it not because they are free of self-concern
that the self is fulfilled?

### Chapter 8

上善若水。
The highest good is like water.

水善利萬物而不爭，處眾人之所惡，
Water benefits all things
and does not contend.
It settles in places others avoid,

故幾於道。
and so comes near the Way.

居善地，心善淵，與善仁，言善信，正善治，事善能，動善時。 夫唯不爭，故無尤。
In dwelling, stay close to the ground.
In mind, remain deep.
In relationship, be generous.
In speech, be true.
In governing, be just.
In action, be capable.
In movement, be timely.
Because it does not contend,
it remains beyond reproach.

### Chapter 9

持而盈之，不如其已； 揣而梲之，不可長保。
Better to stop
than to keep filling to the brim.
Better to temper a blade
than sharpen it past endurance.

金玉滿堂，莫之能守；
When gold and jade fill the hall,
no one can keep them safe.

富貴而驕，自遺其咎。
When wealth and honor breed arrogance,
they leave ruin behind.

功遂身退，天之道。
When the work is done,
withdraw.
This is the Way of Heaven.

### Chapter 10

載營魄抱一，能無離乎？
Can you carry body and soul as one
without letting them drift apart?

專氣致柔，能嬰兒乎？
Can you gather the breath
and become supple as a newborn?

滌除玄覽，能無疵乎？
Can you cleanse the inward vision
until it is free of stain?

愛國治民，能無知乎？
In loving the people and guiding the land,
can you do so without contrivance?

天門開闔，能為雌乎？
As the gates of heaven open and close,
can you remain receptive?

明白四達，能無為乎？
As understanding reaches in every direction,
can you remain unforcing?

生之，
It gives birth,

畜之。
nourishes,

生而不有，為而不恃，長而不宰，是謂玄德。
brings forth without possessing,
acts without relying,
leads without controlling.
This is called profound virtue.

### Chapter 11

三十輻共一轂，當其無，有車之用。
Thirty spokes join at one hub,
but the empty space makes the wheel useful.

埏埴以為器，當其無，有器之用。
Clay is shaped into a vessel,
but the empty space makes the vessel useful.

鑿戶牖以為室，當其無，有室之用。
Doors and windows are cut for a room,
but the empty space makes the room useful.

故有之以為利，無之以為用。
Thus what is there gives form,
and what is not there gives use.

### Chapter 12

五色令人目盲，五音令人耳聾，五味令人口爽，馳騁畋獵令人心發狂，
The five colors blind the eye,
the five tones deafen the ear,
the five flavors dull the tongue.
Racing and hunting
drive the heart toward frenzy.

難得之貨令人行妨。
Rare goods
lead the way astray.

是以聖人為腹不為目，故去彼取此。
Therefore, the unforced leader
cares for the belly, not the eye.
They let go of one
and keep to the other.

### Chapter 13

寵辱若驚，貴大患若身。 何謂寵辱若驚？
Favor and disgrace are both unsettling.
Great trouble is felt through the self.
What does it mean
that favor and disgrace are both unsettling?

寵為下，得之若驚，失之若驚，是謂寵辱若驚。 何謂貴大患若身？
Favor lowers you.
To receive it is to be startled;
to lose it is to be startled.
This is what it means.
What does it mean
to feel great trouble through the self?

吾所以有大患者，為吾有身，
I suffer great trouble
because I have a self.

及吾無身，
If I had no self,

吾有何患？
what trouble could reach me?

故貴以身為天下，若可寄天下；
Therefore, one who values the world
as they value the self
may be entrusted with the world;

愛以身為天下，若可託天下。
one who loves the world
as they love the self
may be given its care.

### Chapter 14

視之不見名曰夷，聽之不聞名曰希，搏之不得名曰微。 此三者，不可致詰，故混而為一。 其上不皦，其下不昧。
Look for it and it cannot be seen:
it is called elusive.
Listen for it and it cannot be heard:
it is called rarefied.
Reach for it and it cannot be grasped:
it is called subtle.
These three cannot be fully named,
so they merge into one.
Above, it is not bright.
Below, it is not dark.

繩繩不可名，復歸於無物。 是謂無狀之狀，無物之象，
Endlessly moving,
it cannot be named
and returns again to no-thing.
This is called the shape of the shapeless,
the image of the imageless,

是謂惚恍。
the faint and indeterminate.

迎之不見其首，隨之不見其後。
Face it,
and you do not see its beginning.
Follow it,
and you do not see its end.

執古之道，以御今之有。
Hold to the ancient Way
to move through the present,

能知古始，是謂道紀。
and know the first beginning.
This is called
the guiding thread of the Way.

### Chapter 15

古之善為士者，微妙玄通，深不可識。 夫唯不可識，故強為之容。
Those who were skilled in the Way in ancient times
were subtle, mysterious, profound,
beyond the reach of easy knowing.
Because they could not be fully known,
one can only trace their appearance:

豫焉若冬涉川， 猶兮若畏四鄰，
careful,
as if crossing a winter stream;
cautious,
as if surrounded on every side;

儼兮其若容，渙兮若冰之將釋，敦兮其若樸，曠兮其若谷，混兮其若濁。
reserved, like a guest;
yielding, like ice beginning to melt;
plain, like uncarved wood;
open, like a valley;
unclear, like muddy water.

孰能濁以靜之徐清？
Who can let the muddy settle
until it clears?

孰能安以久動之徐生？
Who can remain at rest
until life slowly stirs?

保此道者不欲盈，
Those who hold to this Way
do not seek fullness.

夫唯不盈，故能蔽不新成。
Because they do not seek fullness,
they can remain worn
without needing to appear complete.

### Chapter 16

致虛極，守靜篤。 萬物並作，吾以觀復。
Bring emptiness to its fullness.
Keep stillness completely.
As the ten thousand things arise together,
watch how they return.

夫物芸芸，各復歸其根。 歸根曰靜，是謂復命。
All things flourish,
then each returns to its root.
Returning to the root is stillness.
This is called returning to life's command.

復命曰常， 知常曰明。
Returning is the constant.
Knowing the constant is clarity.

不知常，妄作凶。 知常容，
Not knowing the constant,
one acts blindly and meets ruin.
Knowing the constant,
one becomes spacious;

容乃公，
spaciousness becomes impartial;

公乃全，
impartiality becomes whole;

全乃天，
wholeness becomes heaven;

天乃道，
heaven becomes the Way;

道乃久，
the Way endures.

沒身不殆。
And to the end of life,
there is no danger.

### Chapter 17

太上，下知有之，
The highest kind of rule
is one the people barely know is there.

其次，親而譽之，
Next comes the ruler
they love and praise.

其次，畏之，
Next comes the ruler
they fear.

其次，侮之。 信不足焉，有不信焉。
Next comes the ruler
they despise.
When trust is not given,
trust is not returned.

悠兮其貴言，功成事遂，百姓皆謂：我自然。
Measured in speech,
when the work is done
and things have found their course,
the people say,
"We did this ourselves."

### Chapter 18

大道廢，有仁義；
When the great Way is abandoned,
benevolence and righteousness appear.

智慧出，有大偽；
When cleverness and knowledge arise,
great hypocrisy follows.

六親不和，有孝慈；
When the six relations lose harmony,
filial devotion and parental kindness appear.

國家昏亂，有忠臣。
When the state falls into disorder,
loyal ministers appear.

### Chapter 19

絕聖棄智，民利百倍；
Cut off sageliness,
abandon cleverness,
and the people will benefit a hundredfold.

絕仁棄義，民復孝慈；
Cut off benevolence,
abandon righteousness,
and the people will return
to natural affection.

絕巧棄利，盜賊無有。
Cut off skillfulness,
abandon profit,
and thieves will disappear.

此三者以為文不足，故令有所屬：見素抱樸，少私寡欲。
These three are not enough by themselves,
so hold to what is deeper:
see the plain,
embrace the uncarved,
reduce self-interest,
and make desire small.

### Chapter 20

絕學無憂，唯之與阿，相去幾何？ 善之與惡，相去若何？
Give up learned striving,
and trouble falls away.
How much difference is there
between yes and approval?
How much distance is there
between good and bad?

人之所畏，不可不畏。 荒兮其未央哉！
What others fear
cannot simply be ignored.
Vast is this wilderness,
with no end in sight.

眾人熙熙，如享太牢，如春登臺。 我獨泊兮其未兆，如嬰兒之未孩；
Everyone else is bright with excitement,
as if feasting,
as if climbing a terrace in spring.
I alone am quiet,
without sign,
like an infant
who has not yet smiled.

儽儽兮若無所歸。 眾人皆有餘，而我獨若遺。
Drifting,
as if with nowhere to return.
Everyone else has more than enough.
I alone seem to have been left behind.

我愚人之心也哉！ 沌沌兮，
Mine is the mind of a fool,
uncarved, unsettled.

俗人昭昭，
Ordinary people shine with clarity.

我獨昏昏。
I alone seem dim.

俗人察察，
Ordinary people are sharp and exact.

我獨悶悶。
I alone seem obscure.

澹兮其若海，
Vast,
like the sea;

飂兮若無止。
restless,
as if with no place to stop.

眾人皆有以，
Everyone else seems to have a purpose.

而我獨頑似鄙。
I alone remain simple and unformed.

我獨異於人，而貴食母。
I am different from others
because I value
being fed by the Mother.

### Chapter 21

孔德之容，惟道是從。
The shape of profound virtue
follows only the Way.

道之為物，惟恍惟惚。
As a thing, the Way
is dim and elusive.

惚兮恍兮，其中有象；
Elusive and dim,
yet within it there is image;

恍兮惚兮，其中有物。
dim and elusive,
yet within it there is substance.

窈兮冥兮，其中有精；
Dark and hidden,
yet within it there is essence;

其精甚真，其中有信。
that essence is deeply real.
Within it there is trust.

自今及古，其名不去，
From the present back to the ancient past,
its name has never vanished.

以閱眾甫。
By it,
the beginning of all things is seen.

吾何以知眾甫之狀哉？ 以此。
How do I know the way of all beginnings?
By this.

### Chapter 22

曲則全，
What is bent becomes whole.

枉則直，
What is crooked becomes straight.

窪則盈，
What is hollow becomes full.

敝則新，
What is worn becomes new.

少則得，多則惑。
Having little, one receives.
Having much, one is confused.

是以聖人抱一為天下式。
Therefore, the unforced leader
embraces the One
and becomes a pattern for the world.

不自見故明，
Not displaying the self,
they are clear.

不自是故彰，
Not insisting on the self,
they are revealed.

不自伐故有功，
Not boasting,
they have accomplishment.

不自矜故長。
Not exalting themselves,
they endure.

夫唯不爭，故天下莫能與之爭。
Because they do not contend,
none can contend with them.

古之所謂曲則全者，豈虛言哉！
The ancient saying
"What is bent becomes whole"
is not empty speech.

誠全而歸之。
In true wholeness,
all returns.

### Chapter 23

希言自然。
Few words accord with what is natural.

故飄風不終朝，驟雨不終日。
A whirlwind does not last through the morning.
A sudden rain does not last through the day.

孰為此者？
Who does this?

天地。
Heaven and earth.

天地尚不能久，而況於人乎？
If heaven and earth cannot sustain such force,
how much less can human beings?

故從事於道者，道者同於道，
Therefore, one who lives in the Way
becomes one with the Way.

德者同於德，
One who lives in virtue
becomes one with virtue.

失者同於失。
One who lives in loss
becomes one with loss.

同於道者，道亦樂得之；
One with the Way,
the Way receives.

同於德者，德亦樂得之；
One with virtue,
virtue receives.

同於失者，失亦樂得之。
One with loss,
loss receives.

信不足焉，有不信焉。
When trust is insufficient,
there is no trust in return.

### Chapter 24

企者不立，
One who stands on tiptoe
cannot stand firmly.

跨者不行，自見者不明，自是者不彰，自伐者無功，自矜者不長。
One who strides too far
cannot walk well.
One who displays the self
is not clear.
One who asserts the self
is not revealed.
One who boasts
has no accomplishment.
One who exalts the self
does not endure.

其在道也，曰餘食贅行。
Seen from the Way,
these are leftovers and excrescences.

物或惡之，故有道者不處。
Things resist them.
Therefore, one who lives by the Way
does not dwell there.

### Chapter 25

有物混成，先天地生。
There is something formless and complete,
born before heaven and earth.

寂兮寥兮，獨立而不改，
Silent. Empty.
Standing alone, unchanged.

周行而不殆，可以為天下母。
Moving everywhere,
never exhausted.
It may be taken
as the mother of the world.

吾不知其名， 字之曰道，
I do not know its name.
I call it the Way.

強為之名曰大。
Forced to name it further,
I call it great.

大曰逝，
Great means flowing onward.

逝曰遠，遠曰反。
Flowing onward means going far.
Going far means returning.

故道大，天大，地大，王亦大。
So the Way is great.
Heaven is great.
Earth is great.
And the ruler is also great.

域中有四大，
Within the realm
there are four greatnesses,

而王居其一焉。
and the ruler is one of them.

人法地，地法天，天法道，道法自然。
Humanity follows earth.
Earth follows heaven.
Heaven follows the Way.
The Way follows what is naturally so.

### Chapter 26

重為輕根，靜為躁君。
The heavy is the root of the light.
Stillness is the ruler of restlessness.

是以聖人終日行不離輜重。
Therefore, the unforced leader
travels all day
without leaving the weight behind.

雖有榮觀，燕處超然。
Though there are splendid sights,
they remain at ease, above them.

奈何萬乘之主，而以身輕天下？
How can the ruler of ten thousand chariots
take the self lightly before the world?

輕則失本，躁則失君。
Lightness loses the root.
Restlessness loses command.

### Chapter 27

善行無轍迹，
The skillful traveler
leaves no tracks.

善言無瑕讁；
The skillful speaker
leaves no openings for blame.

善數不用籌策；
The skillful reckoner
needs no counting rods.

善閉無關楗而不可開，善結無繩約而不可解。
The skillful closer uses no bolt,
yet what is closed cannot be opened.
The skillful binder uses no cord,
yet what is bound cannot be undone.

是以聖人常善救人，故無棄人；
Therefore, the unforced leader
is always skillful at saving people,
and so abandons no one.

常善救物，故無棄物，是謂襲明。 故善人者，不善人之師；
Always skillful at saving things,
and so abandons nothing.
This is called hidden illumination.
The good person
is the teacher of the not-good;

不善人者，善人之資。
the not-good person
is the material of the good.

不貴其師，不愛其資，雖智大迷，
If the teacher is not honored
and the material is not cared for,
even the intelligent go deeply astray.

是謂要妙。
This is called
the essential mystery.

### Chapter 28

知其雄，守其雌，為天下谿。
Know the masculine,
keep to the feminine,
and become the valley of the world.

為天下谿，常德不離，復歸於嬰兒。
Being the valley of the world,
constant virtue does not leave,
and one returns to the infant.

知其白，守其黑，為天下式。
Know the bright,
keep to the dark,
and become the pattern of the world.

為天下式，常德不忒，
Being the pattern of the world,
constant virtue does not fail,

復歸於無極。
and one returns to the boundless.

知其榮，守其辱，為天下谷，常德乃足，復歸於樸。
Know glory,
keep to humility,
and become the valley of the world.
Then constant virtue is enough,
and one returns to the uncarved block.

樸散則為器，聖人用之，則為官長，
When the uncarved block is divided,
it becomes instruments.
When the unforced leader uses it,
it becomes leadership.

故大制不割。
Thus the great shaping
does not cut.

### Chapter 29

將欲取天下而為之，吾見其不得已。
Whoever would seize the world
and shape it by force,
I see will not succeed.

天下神器，
The world is a sacred vessel.

不可為也，為者敗之，執者失之。
It cannot be forced.
Those who force it spoil it.
Those who grasp it lose it.

故物或行或隨，或歔或吹。 或強或羸，或挫或隳。 是以聖人去甚，去奢，去泰。
Things move ahead or follow behind,
breathe gently or violently,
grow strong or weak,
rise up or collapse.
Therefore, the unforced leader
lets go of excess,
lets go of extravagance,
lets go of overreach.

### Chapter 30

以道佐人主者，不以兵強天下。
One who serves a ruler through the Way
does not use force of arms
to master the world.

其事好還。
Such things return.

師之所處，荊棘生焉。 大軍之後，必有凶年。
Where armies camp,
thorns and brambles grow.
After great war,
lean years follow.

善有果而已，不敢以取強。
A skillful one
achieves what must be achieved
and stops.
They do not use it
to seize power.

果而勿矜，果而勿伐，果而勿驕。
Achieve,
but do not flaunt it.
Achieve,
but do not boast.
Achieve,
but do not become arrogant.

果而不得已，果而勿強。
Achieve

Achieve,
but do not force.

物壯則老，是謂不道，不道早已。
What grows strong
also grows old.
This is not the Way.
What is not the Way

### Chapter 31

夫佳兵者，不祥之器，物或惡之，故有道者不處。
Even fine weapons

Things resist them.
Therefore, one who lives by the Way
does not dwell with them.

君子居則貴左，用兵則貴右。
In ordinary life,
the left is honored.
In war,
the right is honored.

兵者不祥之器，非君子之器，不得已而用之，恬淡為上。
Weapons are instruments of ill omen,
not the tools of one who is grounded.
Use them only when there is no choice.
Best is calm restraint.

勝而不美，而美之者，是樂殺人。
Do not celebrate victory.
To celebrate it
is to delight in killing.

夫樂殺人者，則不可以得志於天下矣。
One who delights in killing
cannot fulfill their will in the world.

吉事尚左，凶事尚右。
On auspicious occasions,
honor the left.
On occasions of mourning,
honor the right.

偏將軍居左，上將軍居右，言以喪禮處之。
The lesser commander stands on the left.
The greater commander stands on the right.
This is the order of mourning.

殺人之眾，以哀悲泣之，戰勝，以喪禮處之。
When many have been killed,
meet it with grief.
When victory is won,
observe the rites of mourning.

### Chapter 32

道常無名，樸雖小，天下莫能臣也。 侯王若能守之，萬物將自賓。
The Way is always without name.
Though the uncarved block seems small,
nothing in the world can master it.
If rulers could hold to it,
all things would come of themselves.

天地相合，以降甘露，民莫之令而自均。
Heaven and earth would join
and sweet dew would fall.
No one would command it,
yet it would be shared by all.

始制有名，名亦既有，夫亦將知止，知止所以不殆。
When carving begins,
names appear.
Once names appear,
know where to stop.
Knowing where to stop
keeps one from danger.

譬道之在天下，猶川谷之於江海。
The Way in the world
is like rivers and valleys
flowing into the great sea.

### Chapter 33

知人者智，自知者明。
To know others is intelligence.
To know yourself is clarity.

勝人者有力，自勝者強。
To overcome others requires force.
To overcome yourself is strength.

知足者富。
To know enough is richness.

強行者有志。 不失其所者久。
To keep going with strength is resolve.
Not losing your place is endurance.

死而不亡者壽。
To die without vanishing
is long life.

### Chapter 34

大道氾兮，其可左右。 萬物恃之而生而不辭，功成不名有。
The great Way flows everywhere,
to the left and to the right.
All things depend on it for life,
and it does not refuse them.
Its work is done,
yet it does not claim possession.

衣養萬物而不為主，常無欲，可名於小；
It clothes and nourishes all things
without becoming their lord.
Always without desire,
it may be called small.

萬物歸焉而不為主，可名為大。
All things return to it,
yet it does not become their lord.
Thus it may be called great.

以其終不自為大，故能成其大。
Because it never makes itself great,
it fulfills its greatness.

### Chapter 35

執大象，天下往。
Hold to the great image,
and the world will come.

往而不害，安平太。
It comes without harm,
and rests in peace.

樂與餌，過客止。
Music and rich food
may stop the traveler for a while.

道之出口，淡乎其無味，視之不足見，聽之不足聞，用之不足既。
But the Way as it is spoken
seems bland and without flavor.
Look at it:
there is not enough to see.
Listen to it:
there is not enough to hear.
Use it:
it cannot be exhausted.

### Chapter 36

將欲歙之，必固張之； 將欲弱之，必固強之；
What is to be closed
must first be opened wide.
What is to be weakened
must first be strengthened.

將欲廢之，必固興之；
What is to be cast down
must first be raised up.

將欲奪之，必固與之。
What is to be taken
must first be given.

是謂微明。
This is called subtle clarity.

柔弱勝剛強。
The soft and weak
overcome the hard and strong.

魚不可脫於淵，國之利器不可以示人。
Fish should not be taken from the deep.
The sharp instruments of a state
should not be displayed.

### Chapter 37

道常無為
The Way is always unforcing,

而無不為。
yet nothing is left undone.

侯王若能守之，萬物將自化。 化而欲作，吾將鎮之以無名之樸。
If rulers could hold to it,
all things would transform themselves.
If, in transforming, desire begins to rise,
I would steady it
with the nameless uncarved block.

無名之樸，夫亦將無欲。
The nameless uncarved block
frees one from desire.

不欲以靜，天下將自定。
Without desire,
in stillness,
the world settles itself.

### Chapter 38

上德不德，是以有德；
The highest virtue does not perform virtue, and so it is truly virtuous.

下德不失德，是以無德。
Lower virtue cannot stop clinging to virtue, and so it lacks true virtue.

上德無為而無以為；
Higher virtue acts without forcing and without agenda.

上仁為之而無以為；
Higher benevolence acts, yet still without agenda.

上義為之而有以為；
Higher righteousness acts with a purpose.

上禮為之而莫之應，則攘臂而扔之。
Higher ritual acts, and when no one responds, it rolls up its sleeves and imposes itself.

故失道而後德，失德而後仁，失仁而後義，失義而後禮。
So when the Way is lost, virtue appears; when virtue is lost, benevolence appears; then righteousness; then ritual.

夫禮者，忠信之薄，而亂之首。
Ritual is the thinning of trust and sincerity, and the beginning of disorder.

前識者，道之華，而愚之始。
Foreknowledge is the flower of the Way, and the beginning of folly.

是以大丈夫處其厚，不居其薄；
So the grounded person dwells in depth, not surface;

處其實，不居其華。
in substance, not appearance;

故去彼取此。
and leaves the latter for the former.

### Chapter 39

昔之得一者，
Of old, those who attained the One:

天得一以清，地得一以寧，神得一以靈，谷得一以盈，萬物得一以生，侯王得一以為天下貞。
heaven became clear;
earth became steady;
spirit became responsive;
the valley became full;
the ten thousand things came to life;
rulers became a measure for the world.

其致之，
This comes from the One.

天無以清將恐裂，
Without clarity,
heaven would split.

地無以寧將恐發，神無以靈將恐歇，谷無以盈將恐竭，萬物無以生將恐滅，侯王無以貴高將恐蹶。
Without steadiness,
earth would break apart.
Without responsiveness,
spirit would fade.
Without fullness,
the valley would run dry.
Without life,
the ten thousand things would perish.
Without humility beneath their height,
rulers would fall.

故貴以賤為本，高以下為基。
Dignity has the low as its root.
Height has the low as its foundation.

是以侯王自稱孤﹑寡﹑不穀。
Thus rulers call themselves
orphaned,
bereft,
unworthy.

此非以賤為本邪？
Is this not taking the low
as the root?

非乎？
Is it not?

故致數輿無輿，不欲琭琭如玉，珞珞如石。
So seek neither the polished gleam of jade
nor the sharp display of ornament.
Be as plain as stone.

### Chapter 40

反者道之動，
Returning
is the movement of the Way.

弱者道之用。
Yielding
is the function of the Way.

天下萬物生於有，有生於無。
All things under heaven
are born from being.
Being is born from non-being.

### Chapter 41

上士聞道，勤而行之； 中士聞道，若存若亡；
When the highest person hears the Way,
they practice it with care.
When the middling person hears the Way,
it seems present, then absent.

下士聞道，大笑之。 不笑不足以為道。
When the lowest person hears the Way,
they laugh aloud.
If it were not laughed at,
it would hardly be the Way.

故建言有之： 明道若昧，
So the old saying has it:
The bright Way seems dim.

進道若退，
The advancing Way seems to withdraw.

夷道若纇， 上德若谷， 大白若辱， 廣德若不足，
The level Way seems rough.
Highest virtue seems like a valley.
Great whiteness seems stained.
Vast virtue seems insufficient.

建德若偷，
Established virtue seems yielding.

質真若渝，
Solid truth seems changeable.

大方無隅，
The great square has no corners.

大器晚成，
The great vessel is slow to complete.

大音希聲， 大象無形，
Great sound is barely heard.
Great form has no shape.

道隱無名。 夫唯道，善貸且成。
The Way is hidden and nameless.
Yet only the Way

### Chapter 42

道生一，一生二，二生三，三生萬物。
The Way gives birth to one.
One gives birth to two.
Two give birth to three.
Three give birth to the ten thousand things.

萬物負陰而抱陽，沖氣以為和。
The ten thousand things
carry yin on their backs

Their harmony

人之所惡，唯孤﹑寡﹑不穀，而王公以為稱。
What people hate

bereft,
unworthy.
Yet rulers take these names for themselves.

故物或損之而益，或益之而損。
So some are diminished
and thereby increased.
Some are increased
and thereby diminished.

人之所教，我亦教之。
What others teach,
I also teach:

強梁者不得其死，吾將以為教父。
the violent and forceful
do not die naturally.
I take this

### Chapter 43

天下之至柔，馳騁天下之至堅。
The softest thing in the world

What has no substance

From this I know

The teaching without words,
the benefit of unforced action,

Which is closer:

Know enough,

Then you can endure.

### Chapter 45

### Chapter 49

聖人無常心，以百姓心為心。 善者，吾善之；
The unforced leader has no fixed mind.

To the good,

To the not-good,

德善。

The unforced leader

The people
fix their eyes and ears on them,

聖人皆孩之。
and the unforced leader
meets them all as children.

### Chapter 50

出生入死。
Coming forth is life.
Entering again is death.

Of ten,
three are inclined toward life,
three toward death,
and three move into death
through the force of living.
Why?
Because they cling too tightly to life.

蓋聞善攝生者，陸行不遇兕虎，入軍不被甲兵； 兕無所投其角，虎無所措其爪，兵無所容其刃。 夫何故？ 以其無死地。
I have heard that one skilled in tending life
walks the land
without meeting rhinoceros or tiger,
enters battle
without needing armor.
The rhinoceros finds no place
to drive its horn.
The tiger finds no place
to fix its claws.
The blade finds no place
to enter.
Why?
Because in such a one
there is no place for death.

### Chapter 51

道生之，德畜之，物形之，勢成之。
The Way gives them life.
Virtue nourishes them.
Things give them form.
Circumstance brings them to completion.

是以萬物莫不尊道而貴德。
So the ten thousand things
all honor the Way
and value virtue.

道之尊，德之貴，夫莫之命而常自然。 故道生之，德畜之。 長之育之，亭之毒之，蓋之覆之。
The Way is honored
and virtue valued
not by command
but because it is naturally so.
So the Way gives life,
virtue nourishes,
raises, develops,
shelters, completes,
covers and protects.

生而不有，為而不恃，
Giving birth without possessing,
acting without relying,

長而不宰。
leading without controlling,

是謂玄德。
this is called profound virtue.

### Chapter 52

天下有始，以為天下母。 既得其母，以知其子，既知其子，復守其母，沒身不殆。
The world has a beginning.
This may be called the Mother of the world.
Once you know the Mother,
you know the children.
Once you know the children,
return to keep to the Mother,
and to the end of life
you will not be endangered.

塞其兌，閉其門，
Close the openings.
Shut the gates.

終身不勤。
And all your life
you will not be worn out.

開其兌，濟其事，終身不救。
Open the openings.
Busy yourself with affairs.
And all your life
there will be no rescue.

見小曰明，守柔曰強。
To see the small is clarity.
To keep to the soft is strength.

用其光，
Use the light,

復歸其明，
return to clarity,

無遺身殃，是為習常。
and leave no disaster for yourself.
This is practicing the constant.

### Chapter 53

使我介然有知，行於大道，唯施是畏。 大道甚夷，而民好徑。
If I had even a little knowing

my only fear

The great Way is smooth,
yet people love side paths.

朝甚除，
The courts are polished bright,

田甚蕪，倉甚虛；

服文綵，帶利劍，厭飲食，財貨有餘；

是為盜夸。

非道也哉！
This is not the Way.

### Chapter 54

善建者不拔，

What is well held

Children and grandchildren

Cultivate it in the world,

the family through the family,
the village through the village,
the state through the state,

以天下觀天下。
the world through the world.

吾何以知天下然哉？
How do I know the world is so?

以此。
By this.

### Chapter 55

含德之厚，比於赤子。
One filled with abundant virtue

蜂蠆虺蛇不螫，猛獸不據，攫鳥不搏。
Wasps and scorpions do not sting.

Bones are weak.
Sinews are soft.

知常曰明。
Knowing the constant is clarity.

益生曰祥。
Forcing life is ill omen.

What grows strong grows old.
This is not the Way.
What is not the Way

### Chapter 56

不可得而利，不可得而害；
cannot be benefited

不可得而貴，不可得而賤。
cannot be raised up

故為天下貴。
Therefore, such a one

### Chapter 57

以正治國，以奇用兵，以無事取天下。 吾何以知其然哉？

Use surprise in war.

How do I know this is so?

By this:

"I practice unforced action,

I love stillness,
and the people become upright.
I do not meddle,
and the people prosper.
I am free of desire,
and the people return

### Chapter 58

When government is muted,

廉而不劌，

straight

bright

### Chapter 59

治人事天，莫若嗇。 夫唯嗇，是謂早服； 早服謂之重積德；
In guiding people and serving heaven,

### Chapter 60

治大國，若烹小鮮。 以道莅天下，其鬼不神；
Governing a great state

When the world is guided by the Way,

Not only do their powers not harm people,
the unforced leader does not harm them either.

夫兩不相傷，故德交歸焉。
When neither harms the other,
virtue returns to both.

### Chapter 61

大國者下流， 天下之交。 天下之牝，
A great state is the low ground
where all streams meet,
the feminine of the world.

牝常以靜勝牡，以靜為下。
The feminine
overcomes the masculine through stillness.
Through stillness
it takes the lower place.

故大國以下小國，
So when a great state
takes the lower place before a small state,

則取小國；
it wins the small state.

小國以下大國，則取大國。
When a small state
takes the lower place before a great state,
it wins the great state.

故或下以取，或下而取。
So some take by lowering themselves,
and some are taken by lowering themselves.

大國不過欲兼畜人，小國不過欲入事人。
A great state wants only
to gather and nourish people.
A small state wants only
to enter and serve.

夫兩者各得其所欲，大者宜為下。
If both are to get what they want,
the great state should take the lower place.

### Chapter 62

道者萬物之奧。
The Way is the hidden depth
of the ten thousand things.

善人之寶，
It is the treasure of the good,

不善人之所保。
and the refuge of the not-good.

美言可以市，尊行可以加人。
Beautiful words can buy honor.
Noble actions can raise a person.

人之不善，何棄之有？ 故立天子，置三公，
But if a person is not good,
why cast them aside?
Therefore, when the Son of Heaven is enthroned
and the three ministers are appointed,

雖有拱璧以先駟馬，不如坐進此道。 古之所以貴此道者何？
though one offers jade before a team of horses,
it is not equal
to offering this Way while seated.
Why did the ancients prize this Way so highly?

不曰以求得，有罪以免邪？
Was it not because
those who seek find,
and those with guilt are spared?

故為天下貴。
Therefore it is precious to the world.

### Chapter 63

為無為，事無事，味無味。
Practice unforced action.
Handle what does not call attention to itself.
Taste what has no flavor.

大小多少，報怨以德。
See the small as large,
the few as many.
Meet injury with virtue.

圖難於其易，為大於其細； 天下難事必作於易，天下大事必作於細。
Approach the difficult
while it is still easy.
Do the great
while it is still small.
All difficult things in the world
begin in what is easy.
All great things in the world
begin in what is small.

是以聖人終不為大，故能成其大。 夫輕諾必寡信，多易必多難。
Therefore, the unforced leader
never tries to do what is great,
and so accomplishes greatness.
Light promises
bring little trust.
Taking too much as easy
brings many difficulties.

是以聖人猶難之，
Therefore, the unforced leader
takes even the easy as difficult,

故終無難矣
and so in the end
has no difficulties.

### Chapter 64

其安易持，其未兆易謀。
What is at rest
is easy to hold.
What has not yet shown itself
is easy to plan for.

其脆易泮，其微易散。
What is brittle
is easy to break.
What is slight
is easy to scatter.

為之於未有，
Act before something exists.

治之於未亂。 合抱之木，生於毫末；
Set things in order
before disorder begins.
A tree that fills the arms
grows from the tiniest shoot.

九層之臺，起於累土； 千里之行，始於足下。
A nine-story tower
rises from a heap of earth.
A thousand-mile journey
begins beneath the foot.

為者敗之，執者失之。
Those who force things
ruin them.
Those who grasp them
lose them.

是以聖人無為故無敗，無執故無失。
Therefore, the unforced leader
does not force
and so does not fail.
Does not grasp
and so does not lose.

民之從事，常於幾成而敗之。
People in handling affairs
often fail
when they are close to completion.

慎終如始，則無敗事。
If they were as careful at the end
as at the beginning,
there would be no failures.

是以聖人欲不欲，不貴難得之貨；
Therefore, the unforced leader
desires not to desire
and does not prize rare goods.

學不學，復眾人之所過。
They learn what is not learned
and return to what others have passed by.

以輔萬物之自然，而不敢為。
In this way
they support the naturalness of all things
and do not dare to force.

### Chapter 65

古之善為道者，非以明民，將以愚之。 民之難治，以其智多。
Those skilled in the Way in ancient times
did not use it
to fill the people with cleverness,
but to keep them simple.
People are hard to govern
because they have too much cleverness.

故以智治國，國之賊， 不以智治國，國之福。
To govern a state through cleverness
is to rob the state.
To govern a state
without cleverness
is to bless it.

知此兩者亦稽式。 常知稽式，是謂玄德。
Knowing these two
is itself a pattern.
To keep to this pattern
is called profound virtue.

玄德深矣，遠矣，
Profound virtue is deep.
It reaches far.

與物反矣，
It seems to move against the grain of things,

然後乃至大順。
and thereby arrives
at great harmony.

### Chapter 66

江海所以能為百谷王者，以其善下之，故能為百谷王。 是以欲上民，必以言下之。 欲先民，必以身後之。
Rivers and seas
become kings of a hundred valleys
because they are skilled
at staying below them.
So if you would stand above the people,
you must speak from below them.
If you would lead the people,
you must place yourself behind them.

是以聖人處上而民不重，處前而民不害。 是以天下樂推而不厭，以其不爭，故天下莫能與之爭。
Therefore, the unforced leader
stands above
without weighing people down,
goes ahead
without standing in their way.
So the world
gladly supports them
and does not tire of them.
Because they do not contend,
none can contend with them.

### Chapter 67

天下皆謂我道大，似不肖。 夫唯大，故似不肖。
All the world says
my Way is great,
and because it is great
it seems unlike anything familiar.

若肖，久矣其細也夫！ 我有三寶，持而保之。
If it were familiar,
it would long ago
have been small.
I have three treasures.
I hold to them
and keep them.

一曰慈，二曰儉，三曰不敢為天下先。 慈故能勇，
The first is compassion.
The second is frugality.
The third is not daring
to place oneself before the world.
Through compassion,
one can be brave.

儉故能廣，
Through frugality,
one can be generous.

不敢為天下先，故能成器長。
By not daring
to place oneself before the world,
one can become a vessel of leadership.

今舍慈且勇，
Now if one abandons compassion
and tries to be brave,

舍儉且廣，舍後且先，死矣！
abandons frugality
and tries to be expansive,
abandons the rear
and tries to be first,
that is death.

夫慈以戰則勝， 以守則固。
Through compassion in battle,
one wins.
Through compassion in defense,
one stands firm.

天將救之，以慈衛之。
When heaven would save someone,
it protects them
with compassion.

### Chapter 68

善為士者不武，
One skilled as a warrior
is not warlike.

善戰者不怒，
One skilled in battle
is not angry.

善勝敵者不與，
One skilled at overcoming an enemy
does not engage in hatred.

善用人者為之下，是謂不爭之德，是謂用人之力， 是謂配天古之極。
One skilled in using people
places themselves below them.
This is called
the virtue of not contending.
This is called
the power of using people.
This is called
matching heaven,
the highest attainment of old.

### Chapter 69

用兵有言：「吾不敢為主而為客，不敢進寸而退尺。
There is a saying in the use of arms:
"I do not dare to be the host.
I choose to be the guest.
I do not dare advance an inch.
I prefer to retreat a foot."

」是謂行無行，
This is called
marching without marching,

攘無臂，扔無敵，
rolling up sleeves without arms,
grappling without an enemy,

執無兵。
grasping without weapons.

禍莫大於輕敵，輕敵幾喪吾寶。
No disaster is greater
than taking the enemy lightly.
To take the enemy lightly
is nearly to lose my treasures.

故抗兵相加，哀者勝矣。
So when opposing armies meet,
the grieving side prevails.

### Chapter 70

吾言甚易知，甚易行。 天下莫能知，莫能行。
My words are easy to understand
and easy to practice.
Yet no one in the world
understands them
or practices them.

言有宗，事有君。
Words have an origin.
Actions have a guiding thread.

夫唯無知，是以不我知。
Because people do not know this,
they do not know me.

知我者希，則我者貴。
Those who know me are few.
Therefore I am precious.

是以聖人被褐懷玉。
Therefore, the unforced leader
wears coarse cloth
and carries jade within.

### Chapter 71

知不知上，不知知病。
To know not-knowing
is highest.
Not to know
yet think you know
is sickness.

夫唯病病，是以不病。
Only by being sick of this sickness
can one be free of it.

聖人不病，以其病病，是以不病。
The unforced leader is free of sickness
because they are sick of sickness.
And so they are not sick.

### Chapter 72

民不畏威，則大威至。 無狎其所居，無厭其所生。 夫唯不厭，
When people no longer fear power,
greater power arrives.
Do not press them in their living.
Do not weary them in their lives.
Only when they are not wearied

是以不厭。
will they not turn weary.

是以聖人自知不自見，
Therefore, the unforced leader
knows the self
but does not display the self,

自愛不自貴；
loves the self
but does not exalt the self.

故去彼取此。
So they leave one behind
and keep to the other.

### Chapter 73

勇於敢則殺，
Courage in daring
leads to death.

勇於不敢則活。
Courage in not daring
leads to life.

此兩者，或利或害。
Of these two,
one helps,
one harms.

天之所惡，孰知其故？ 是以聖人猶難之。 天之道，不爭而善勝，
What heaven rejects,
who knows the reason?
Therefore, even the unforced leader
finds it difficult.
The Way of heaven
does not contend,
yet it prevails.

不言而善應， 不召而自來，
It does not speak,
yet responds well.
It is not summoned,
yet comes of itself.

繟然而善謀。
Still and spacious,
yet skillful in design.

天網恢恢，疏而不失。
The net of heaven
is vast and loose,
yet nothing slips through.

### Chapter 74

民不畏死，奈何以死懼之？
If people do not fear death,
how can death be used
to frighten them?

若使民常畏死，而為奇者，吾得執而殺之，孰敢？
If people truly feared death,
and I could seize those who act strangely
and kill them,
who would dare?

常有司殺者殺，夫代司殺者殺，是謂代大匠斲。
There is always
one who presides over killing.
To kill in that one's place
is like hewing in place
of a master carpenter.

夫代大匠斲者，希有不傷其手矣。
Those who hew
in place of the master carpenter
rarely escape
without injuring their own hands.

### Chapter 75

民之饑，以其上食稅之多，是以饑。
People starve
because those above them
consume too much in taxes.
That is why they starve.

民之難治，以其上之有為，是以難治。
People are hard to govern
because those above them
do too much.
That is why they are hard to govern.

民之輕死，以其上求生之厚，是以輕死。
People make light of death
because those above them
cling too hard to life.
That is why they make light of death.

夫唯無以生為者，是賢於貴生。
Only those who do not live
for the sake of life
are wiser
than those who prize life too much.

### Chapter 76

人之生也柔弱，其死也堅強。
When alive,
people are soft and yielding.
When dead,
they are hard and rigid.

萬物草木之生也柔脆，其死也枯槁。
The ten thousand things,
the grasses and trees,
are soft and tender in life,
dry and brittle in death.

故堅強者死之徒，柔弱者生之徒。
So hardness and strength
belong to death.
Softness and yielding
belong to life.

是以兵強則不勝，
Therefore,
when an army is strong
it does not prevail,

木強則兵。
and when a tree is strong
it is cut down.

強大處下，
The strong and great
take the lower place.

柔弱處上。
The soft and yielding
take the higher place.

### Chapter 77

天之道，其猶張弓與？ 高者抑之，下者舉之；
Is not the Way of heaven
like drawing a bow?
What is high is pressed down.
What is low is lifted up.

有餘者損之，不足者補之。 天之道，損有餘而補不足。
What has excess is reduced.
What is lacking is supplied.
The Way of heaven
reduces excess
and supplies what is lacking.

人之道則不然，
The human way is not like this.

損不足以奉有餘。
It takes from what lacks
to serve what already has excess.

孰能有餘以奉天下？
Who can have enough
to serve the world?

唯有道者。
Only one who has the Way.

是以聖人為而不恃，功成而不處，其不欲見賢。
Therefore, the unforced leader
acts without relying,
accomplishes without dwelling on accomplishment,
and does not wish
to display superiority.

### Chapter 78

天下莫柔弱於水，而攻堅強者，莫之能勝，其無以易之。
Nothing in the world
is softer or weaker than water,
yet in overcoming what is hard and strong,
nothing can surpass it.
Nothing can take its place.

弱之勝強，柔之勝剛，天下莫不知莫能行。 是以聖人云：「受國之垢，是謂社稷主； 受國不祥，是為天下王。 」正言若反。
The weak overcomes the strong.
The soft overcomes the hard.
Everyone in the world knows this,
yet no one can practice it.
So the unforced leader says:
"One who receives a nation's humiliation
is lord of its altars.
One who receives a nation's misfortune
is king of the world."
True words
seem reversed.

### Chapter 79

和大怨，必有餘怨， 安可以為善？
After a great resentment is reconciled,
some resentment remains.
How can that be called good?

是以聖人執左契，
Therefore, the unforced leader
holds the left side of the tally

而不責於人。 有德司契，
and does not press claims on others.
One with virtue attends to the tally.

無德司徹。
One without virtue
attends only to collection.

天道無親，常與善人。
The Way of heaven
has no favorites.
It always stands with the good.

### Chapter 80

小國寡民，
Let there be a small state
with few people.

使有什伯之器而不用，
Even if there are tools
for ten or a hundred uses,
let them not be used.

使民重死而不遠徙。
Let people take death seriously
and not wander far.

雖有舟輿，無所乘之；
Though there are boats and carriages,
let there be no reason to ride them.

雖有甲兵，無所陳之。
Though there are armor and weapons,
let there be no reason to display them.

使人復結繩而用之，甘其食，美其服，安其居，樂其俗。 鄰國相望，雞犬之聲相聞，民至老死，不相往來。
Let people return
to knotting cords.
Let them find their food sweet,
their clothes beautiful,
their homes peaceful,
their customs joyful.
Neighboring states
may look across at one another.
The sounds of dogs and chickens
may be heard across the distance.
Yet the people grow old and die
without going back and forth.

### Chapter 81

信言不美，
True words are not beautiful.

美言不信。
Beautiful words are not true.

善者不辯，辯者不善。
Those who are good
do not argue.
Those who argue
are not good.

知者不博，
Those who know
are not broadly stuffed with knowledge.

博者不知。
Those broadly stuffed with knowledge
do not know.

聖人不積，
The unforced leader
does not accumulate for the self.

既以為人己愈有，
The more they do for others,
the more they have.

既以與人己愈多。 天之道，利而不害；
The more they give to others,
the more they possess.
The Way of heaven
benefits and does not harm.

聖人之道，為而不爭。
The way of the unforced leader
acts and does not contend.
