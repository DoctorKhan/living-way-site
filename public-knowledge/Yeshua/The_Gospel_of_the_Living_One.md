# **The Gospel of the Living One**

*A poetic Yeshuan narrative shaped from canonical Gospel themes*

> This text is a contemplative retelling, not a translation.
>
> It draws its imagery and movement from the canonical Gospels, especially Matthew, Luke, and John. It stays closer to the inner, sapiential, and mystical stream than to doctrinal summary.

---

## **I. The Nearness of the Kingdom**

And he said to them:

Do not seek first for signs in the sky, nor say in your hearts,
"When the hour comes, then the Kingdom will arrive."

For the Kingdom does not come only in ways that can be measured.
It is near.
It is hidden.
It is already at work.

It is like seed in the earth.
It is like leaven in the dough.
It is like treasure beneath the field no passerby notices.

Those who live by spectacle do not see it.
Those who look only outward do not recognize it.
But the one who turns, and watches, and becomes simple again
will begin to find it.

---

## **II. Become Like a Child**

And the disciples asked who was greatest.

But he placed a child before them and said:

Unless you turn and become as children,
you will not enter the Kingdom.

For the gate is not opened by rank.
It is not opened by mastery.
It is not opened by those who make themselves high.

The one who receives becomes great.
The one who becomes small begins to see.
The one who is no longer defending the self
begins to live from the heart.

---

## **III. Clean the Inside First**

And he said:

You wash the outside and leave the inside untouched.
You honor what is seen and neglect what gives birth to what is seen.

But what defiles a person does not come first from outside.
It rises from the heart:

from deceit,
from grasping,
from envy,
from fear,
from judgment,
from the storms that move unseen within.

Therefore clean the inside first.
Let the cup become whole from within.
For when the root is healed, the fruit changes.

---

## **IV. The Light of Life**

In him was life,
and the life was the light of human beings.

And he said:

If the eye is clear,
the whole body will be full of light.

Therefore attend to the place from which you see.
For if perception is darkened,
the whole world becomes shadow.

But if the eye becomes single,
if the heart becomes undivided,
if the inner lamp is not buried,
then the whole person becomes luminous.

Those who follow the light of life
do not walk forever in darkness.

---

## **V. Spirit and the Living Water**

A woman came to the well,
carrying thirst deeper than the vessel in her hands.

And he said to her:

The hour is coming when the Father
will not be sought only on this mountain
or in that city.

For God is spirit,
and those who worship must worship
in spirit and in truth.

And he said:

The water I give
will become in the one who drinks
a spring welling up to eternal life.

Then the place of worship is no longer only outside.
The spring is opened within.
And from the heart
flow rivers of living water.

---

## **VI. The Treasure and the Pearl**

The Kingdom is like treasure hidden in a field.
The Kingdom is like a pearl of great price.

Many pass over the field and do not know what lies beneath it.
Many hold lesser things and call themselves rich.

But the one who sees truly
gives up what is secondary
for what cannot be measured.

So it is with the soul.
Its true wealth is hidden
until the moment of recognition.

---

## **VII. The Divided Heart**

And he said:

No one can serve two masters.

For the divided heart is always pulled apart.
It says yes with the lips and no within.
It seeks God and clings to what cannot give life.
It desires freedom and loves its chains.

Therefore watch.
Watch the anger before it becomes violence.
Watch the lust before it becomes possession.
Watch the fear before it becomes a world.

Blessed is the one who sees what rises within
before it rules the whole house.

---

## **VIII. Abide in the Word**

And he said:

If you abide in my word,
you will know the truth,
and the truth will make you free.

Freedom is not given to the one who merely repeats words.
Freedom is given to the one who remains,
who abides,
who lets the word descend below the surface
until it becomes life.

Whoever does what is true comes to the light.
Whoever loves the light learns not to hide.

---

## **IX. The Passing from Death to Life**

And he said:

Whoever hears my word
has passed from death to life.

And again:

If anyone keeps my word,
he will not see death.

This is the mystery:
that life begins before the grave,
and death begins before the grave also.

Whoever clings to the false life loses it.
Whoever lets the grain fall into the earth
finds the life that cannot be kept by fear.

Thus the Way is not only consolation for the end.
It is awakening now.

---

## **X. That They May Be One**

And he lifted his eyes and prayed:

That they may all be one,
as you, Father, are in me and I in you.

Not one by force.
Not one by institution alone.
Not one by the sameness of language.

But one by indwelling,
one by love,
one by participation in the life that is from the Father.

This is the goal:
not merely to belong,
but to abide;
not merely to obey,
but to become transparent to divine life.

---

## **XI. The Living Way**

So the Living Way, in the canonical witness, may be heard like this:

The Kingdom is near.
Become like a child.
Clean the inside first.
Let the eye become full of light.
Drink from the spring within.
Choose the treasure over appearances.
Watch the divided heart.
Abide in the truth.
Pass from death to life.
Become one.

Whoever has ears to hear, let him hear.
